# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Aristoteles is aimed at people who must compare suppliers, contracts, proposals, or support documents and then defend a decision to stakeholders. They arrive with scattered PDFs and images, inconsistent formats, missing data, and pressure to justify the final recommendation.

## Product Purpose

Aristoteles turns private documents into a structured, evidence-backed comparison. It prepares the corpus, proposes criteria, extracts facts, compares alternatives, surfaces risks and contradictions, and produces a non-binding recommendation with citations and a confidence rubric. Success means the user can make the final decision faster while knowing where every material claim came from.

## Brand Personality

Clinical, orbital, accountable. The voice should feel like a mission-control console for evidence: precise, calm, and explicit about uncertainty.

## Anti-references

Do not present the product as a chatbot, an automatic decision maker, or a generic AI assistant. Avoid vague SaaS claims, inflated autonomy, decorative dashboards without traceability, and copy that implies legal, financial, or procurement authority beyond the documented evidence.

## Design Principles

1. Evidence first: factual claims need visible source lineage.
2. Human final control: the interface recommends, but the user decides.
3. Traceable confidence: uncertainty, missing data, and contradictions are product features, not footnotes.
4. Mission-control clarity: dense systems should feel navigable through staged progress and crisp labels.
5. Privacy by default: documents are private artifacts, never marketing scenery.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-readable navigation, meaningful link labels, reduced-motion support, and copy that does not rely only on color to communicate status or risk.
